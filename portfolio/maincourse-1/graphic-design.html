'Eye' – Graphic Design

Fictive assignment of an album cover for a CD.

Description:

EYE has given me the job to create an album cover for their album Awake. I want to show and describe how I've gotten to the finished product, using the customer's input and ideas, and my understanding and execution.

Programmes:

Photoshop
InDesign

Description of customer:

The customer is a death metal band called EYE, and Awake is their newest album. To make Awake the next big hit of death metal, they've contacted me to show the world what they have to offer.

Inspiration and brainstorm:

The customer came up with a lot of ideas for content, colors and fonts to use. Since I can't just make something of 'being awake', the expression was also reviewed.

Sketches

After the first meeting with the customer, I could start sketching. The album was described with keywords such as gloomy, dark, insomnia, paranoia. I felt like a face expressing those things fits the description. And the black eyes, aka. 'devil eyes', to weight towards the dark and gloomy.

Colors:

The customer's colors of choice were red and black. The blue color comes from the red, as I inverted it in the glitch effects. I picked out a darker red because it looks so good with the black. The customer approved.

Font:

I used 'Evil Dead' as the font. It's a display font that looks like a sans-serif, with discreet serifs in various places. It reminds me a bit of Copperplate. Even though it's a display font, it's easy to read and looks good with the logo.

Cerebration of elements:

Every element has a thread of consistency. The glitch effect is the glue to it all, one could say. Besides creating some contrast to the red, it also fills out a lot of space, without making it sound like it was just dumped in there. The red elements are clearly the primary elements, because it's the primary color and creates more contrast than the blue does. I've made sure the red and blue colors are in harmony. It can be seen especially on the back of the cover, where the face is blue, and the album title, song list and logo pops out with a clear contrast.

The artwork of the inside of the back is moved slightly to the right, so it looks the same with or without the CD.
